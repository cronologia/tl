Óscar Romero

1917–1980 · El Salvador

Óscar Arnulfo Romero (1917–1980), Archbishop of San Salvador from 1977, the era's most prominent Salvadoran church voice against state repression before his assassination while saying Mass on 24 March 1980. Canonized in 2018 as a martyr killed in odium fidei, not as a liberation theologian; his own relationship to the current is debated among biographers.[1] [2] [3] [4]

YearDateEvent
1977 1977-03-12 Murder of Rutilio Grande — Jesuit priest Rutilio Grande, an advocate of peasant organizing and a friend of the newly appointed Archbishop Romero, was ambushed and killed with two companions. Many biographers (following Brockman) treat the killing as the catalyst of Romero's public turn; others (Morozzo della Rocca) argue his stance evolved from his pastoral formation. Both readings are attributed.[5] [6] [7] [8]
1980 1980-03-24 Assassination of Óscar Romero — A day after a homily ordering soldiers, 'in the name of God', to cease the repression, Romero was shot dead while celebrating Mass. The 1993 UN Truth Commission for El Salvador found that former Major Roberto D'Aubuisson had given the order to kill the archbishop; the finding is attributed to that report.[3] [2] [9] [1]
2015 2015-02-03 Romero recognized as a martyr — Pope Francis authorized a decree recognizing that Romero was killed 'in hatred of the faith' (in odium fidei), declaring him a martyr and clearing the way for beatification without a certified miracle, after years of debate over whether he had died for the faith or for political stances.[4] [2]
2015 2015-05-23 Romero beatified — Óscar Romero is declared a martyr (February 2015) and beatified.[1]
2018 2018-10-14 Romero canonized — Francis canonizes Óscar Romero.[1]
