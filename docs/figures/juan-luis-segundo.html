Juan Luis Segundo

1925–1996 · Uruguay

Juan Luis Segundo (1925–1996), Uruguayan Jesuit theologian in Montevideo and a founding figure of the current. He developed the 'hermeneutic circle' method centred on ideological suspicion, holding that what liberates theology is its methodology rather than its content, and drew less on Marxist social analysis than some peers.[1] [2]

Segundo's 'Theology and the Church' answers Cardinal Ratzinger — Juan Luis Segundo published 'Theology and the Church: A Response to Cardinal Ratzinger and a Warning to the Whole Church', a book-length rebuttal to the CDF's 1984 Instruction 'Libertatis Nuntius', arguing the critique rested on a dualistic theology at odds with Vatican II and missed what liberation theologians actually taught. It became a key primary document of the reception debate (reviewed by Avery Dulles, 1986).[3] [4] [5]
