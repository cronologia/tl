Camilo Torres

1929–1966 · Colombia

Colombian priest and sociologist who co-founded Latin America's first sociology faculty at the Universidad Nacional, led the Frente Unido movement, was laicized in 1965 and joined the ELN guerrilla, dying in his first combat in 1966. A precursor symbol who predates the current, not a liberation theologian: his choice of armed struggle was rejected by its main theologians such as Gustavo Gutiérrez.[1] [2] [3] [4]
