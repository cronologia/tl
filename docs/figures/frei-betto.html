Frei Betto (Carlos Alberto Libânio Christo)

b. 1944 · Brazil

Brazilian Dominican friar, writer and popular educator (b. 1944, Belo Horizonte). Twice jailed under the military dictatorship (1964; c. 1969–1973), he became a leading organizer of Brazil's comunidades eclesiais de base and their interecclesial meetings. Author of 'Fidel e a Religião' (1985) and later a Fome Zero adviser (2003–04); his links to the Workers' Party and Cuba are documented in the sibling fsp project.[1] [2] [3] [4]
