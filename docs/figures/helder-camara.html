Hélder Câmara

1909–1999 · Brazil

Brazilian Catholic prelate, Archbishop of Olinda e Recife (1964–1985) and co-founder of the CNBB (1952). A pastoral 'bishop of the poor' and human-rights advocate under the dictatorship, he is remembered as a precursor and moral inspiration of the current's social-justice strand rather than a systematic theologian; earlier an integralist sympathizer, he later renounced that stance.[1] [2] [3]

1964 1964-03-12 Hélder Câmara named Archbishop of Olinda and Recife — Dom Hélder Câmara was appointed Archbishop of Olinda and Recife on 12 March 1964, weeks before the military coup; from that see he became a leading pastoral voice for the poor and a persistent target of the dictatorship, though he was never a systematic liberation theologian.[2]
1965 1965-11-16 Pact of the Catacombs — Three weeks before Vatican II closed, some 40 bishops celebrated Mass in the Catacombs of Domitilla and signed the 'Pact of the Catacombs', pledging to live simply and make a 'church of the poor' central to their ministry. Hélder Câmara was a leading promoter; over 500 bishops later added their signatures. The pact became closely associated with the emerging current.[4] [5]
1999 1999-08-27 Death of Hélder Câmara — Hélder Câmara died on 27 August 1999, aged 90. As Archbishop of Olinda e Recife he had been an internationally known advocate for the poor and for human rights under the dictatorship — subjected to press censorship (1968–1977) and repeatedly nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize — and is widely regarded as a pastoral precursor of the current.[2] [1]
2015 2015 ? Hélder Câmara's canonization cause opened — The Archdiocese of Olinda and Recife opened Câmara's cause and, with the Holy See's assent, he received the title 'Servant of God' — the first formal step toward a possible beatification.[6] [7]
